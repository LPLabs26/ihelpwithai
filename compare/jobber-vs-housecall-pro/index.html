Head-to-head comparison

Jobber vs Housecall Pro

The most common question for field service businesses. The right answer depends on your team size and how you dispatch.

Jobber
  • ✓Faster to set up (hours not weeks)
  • ✓Simpler, cleaner mobile app for techs
  • ✓More transparent pricing
  • ✓Best for 1–10 person teams
  • ✓Easier client communication flow
Housecall Pro
  • ✓Built-in GPS tracking and fleet visibility
  • ✓More powerful dispatch board
  • ✓Consumer financing via Wisetack
  • ✓Better for 5–50+ person teams
  • ✓Stronger reporting and marketing tools
Pricing comparison
Jobber

Solo: $29–$149/mo · Teams: $149–$529/mo · 14-day trial. Pricing is per-tier, not per-seat at lower plans.

Housecall Pro

Basic: $59/mo · Essentials: $149/mo · MAX: $299/mo · 14-day trial. Scales up with team size and features.

The verdict

Choose Jobber if: You have fewer than 10 people, you need to get up and running fast, and you don't have complex dispatch or GPS requirements. Most small trades businesses start here and don't need to leave.

Choose Housecall Pro if: You have 5+ technicians in the field, GPS tracking and a visual dispatch board matter to your operation, or consumer financing is part of how you close jobs.

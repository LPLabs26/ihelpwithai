Comparison

Fathom vs Fireflies vs Otter

Three strong meeting assistants with different strengths in simplicity, workflow follow-up, and searchable notes.

The short answer

Choose Fathom for the easiest fast-start experience, Fireflies for more workflow-oriented team use, and Otter for a simpler transcript-first workflow.

Compare the tools

Meeting workflow Fathom Free to try

Fathom

A very approachable AI meeting assistant for notes, action items, summaries, and cleaner post-call follow-up.

Best for: Best for founders, account teams, operators, and anyone who wants meeting follow-up without a lot of setup.

Real use case: Turn a customer call into a clean summary, a short follow-up email, and a list of next actions for the owner.

Watch-outs: If you need deeper conversation intelligence, coaching, or a larger system around calls, a heavier platform may fit better.

Meeting workflow Fireflies.ai Free to try

Fireflies

A strong meeting assistant for recording calls, pulling summaries, and keeping follow-ups from slipping.

Best for: Best for sales teams, operators, agencies, and client-facing teams in lots of calls.

Real use case: After every discovery call, create a summary, action list, and CRM-ready handoff without doing it by hand.

Watch-outs: It works best when your team is comfortable with meeting recording, permissions, and review workflows.

Meeting workflow Otter.ai Free to try

Otter

A simple meeting note-taker for transcripts, summaries, and searchable call records.

Best for: Best for teams, students, and operators who want less note-taking overhead.

Real use case: Capture a customer interview, then search the transcript later for objections, requests, and next steps.

Watch-outs: If you want more advanced meeting intelligence or partner monetization paths, Fireflies may be stronger.

Which meeting-note tool should you start with first?

Decision rules

  • Choose Fathom if you want useful call notes and follow-up with the least friction.
  • Choose Fireflies if post-meeting workflows, team handoff, and follow-up matter most.
  • Choose Otter if searchable transcripts and straightforward note capture are the priority.

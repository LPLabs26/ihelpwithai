# ihelpwithai strategy

## Core goal
Make learning about and finding AI products easy for people who are not tech savvy.

## Positioning
**Plain-English AI tool recommendations for normal people.**

We should not try to be the biggest AI directory.
We should be the easiest place to:
- understand what a tool does
- know who it is for
- know when to use it
- know when to skip it
- find a safe first tool to try

## What to optimize for
- clarity over hype
- simple language over AI jargon
- curation over volume
- trust over affiliate pressure
- practical jobs-to-be-done over vendor buzz

## Editorial rules
Every page should help a non-technical person answer:
1. What does this tool do?
2. Who is it for?
3. Is it easy to use?
4. What does it cost?
5. What is the catch?
6. What should I try first?

## What competitors do well
- filters
- comparisons
- freshness signals
- long-tail SEO pages

## What competitors do badly
- too many choices
- too much jargon
- trend chasing
- weak beginner guidance
- affiliate-spam feel

## How we win
- best beginner picks
- plain-English explanations
- clear watch-outs
- easier “start here” paths
- task-first guides
- strong comparisons

## Product priorities
1. Homepage should guide beginners fast.
2. Make “best first pick” more obvious.
3. Expand task-first pages.
4. Keep recommendations curated.
5. Make trust/disclosure visible but calm.

## Working principle
If a page would confuse a normal person who just wants help with writing, meetings, research, slides, or video, simplify it.

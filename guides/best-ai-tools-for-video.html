Best AI tools for video

These are the most useful AI tools to compare when video is part of the job and you need speed without a big production budget.

What this guide helps with

Compare these for recording, editing, repurposing, generating, and publishing video faster.

Best tools to compare first

Design workflow Adobe Free to try

Adobe Firefly

A strong design-first AI option for generating and editing creative assets inside a more professional Adobe workflow.

Use it when: Use Adobe Firefly when you want generative design help but still need more control over final creative assets and editing.

Real use case: Create a campaign visual direction, expand the image set, and prepare assets that can move into production quickly.

Read the full review Visit official site
Video workflow Descript Free to try

Descript

Best for editing spoken content because it lets you edit audio and video like a document.

Use it when: Use Descript to record, transcribe, cut filler words, add captions, and publish polished content quickly.

Real use case: Drop in a webinar recording and turn it into cleaned-up clips with captions for social media.

Read the full review Visit official site
Voice workflow ElevenLabs Free to try

ElevenLabs

One of the strongest voice tools for realistic narration, multilingual audio, and voice agents.

Use it when: Use ElevenLabs for text-to-speech, voice cloning, speech-to-text, and voice experiences across channels.

Real use case: Create voiceovers for the same product demo in multiple languages without booking a new recording session each time.

Read the full review Visit official site
Video workflow HeyGen Free to try

HeyGen

A strong option for avatar-led videos, translated talking-head content, and business videos that need to look polished fast.

Use it when: Use HeyGen when you want presenter-style videos without filming each version by hand, especially for sales, onboarding, or multilingual content.

Real use case: Create a personalized sales intro or onboarding explainer in multiple languages without re-recording the presenter.

Read the full review Visit official site
Video workflow Loom Free to try

Loom

A strong first pick for async explanation because it makes screen recording, sharing, and AI summaries feel simple.

Use it when: Use Loom when you need to explain something once on video instead of repeating the same instructions in meetings or long messages.

Real use case: Record a product walkthrough once, then turn it into a shareable update, a summary, and a reusable SOP.

Read the full review Visit official site
Video workflow VEED Free to try

VEED

A browser-based AI video platform for captions, edits, talking-head polish, dubbing, and faster content publishing.

Use it when: Use VEED when the job is getting videos edited, captioned, repurposed, or localized without opening a heavy desktop editor.

Real use case: Turn one webinar clip into a captioned social cut, a training edit, and a dubbed version for another market.

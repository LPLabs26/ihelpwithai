Affiliate Disclosure

How this site makes money, who the affiliate relationships are with, and why they don't change what gets recommended.

The short version

ihelpwithai.com uses affiliate and referral links. When you click a link on this site and sign up for a tool, I may earn a referral fee — at no extra cost to you, and typically at the same price you'd pay going directly to the vendor. Affiliate relationships do not influence which tools are reviewed, how they are rated, or what gets recommended.

What affiliate links are

An affiliate or referral link is a tracked URL. When you click it and complete a purchase or signup, the vendor's system records that the referral came from this site and pays a commission. The commission comes from the vendor's marketing budget — it does not add to your price.

This is a standard practice used by most product review sites, comparison tools, and recommendation platforms across the internet. The FTC requires disclosure of these relationships, which is what this page is.

How recommendations are made

Every recommendation on this site is based on fit — business type, problem being solved, team size, setup tolerance, and realistic cost. Tools are not recommended because they pay higher commissions. Tools are not excluded because they don't have an affiliate program.

The personal recommendation service ($97 written consult) follows the same standard. The fee you pay is for the recommendation itself. Whether or not an affiliate link is used in the follow-up does not change what gets recommended.

If this site recommended bad-fit tools for commission, it would stop being useful. A site that isn't useful doesn't generate traffic, trust, or revenue. The incentives align with honest recommendations.

FTC compliance

This disclosure is made in accordance with the Federal Trade Commission's guidelines on endorsements and testimonials (16 CFR Part 255). Affiliate relationships are disclosed on every page where affiliate links appear via a footer note or inline disclosure.

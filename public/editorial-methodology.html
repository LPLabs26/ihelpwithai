Editorial trust

How ihelpwithai.com reviews tools

The goal is not to catalog everything in AI. The goal is to help someone choose faster with enough context to avoid bad-fit tools.

What makes a tool worth including

  • It solves a recurring job someone can explain in plain English.
  • It earns a clear recommendation for a defined audience, not everybody.
  • It has visible tradeoffs, limits, or watch-outs worth surfacing.
  • It belongs in a shortlist a buyer could realistically compare this week.

What goes into each review

Fit first

Each review explains what the tool is for, who should use it, and one real use case.

Tradeoffs stay visible

Every tool keeps watch-outs visible so the page does not become a sales pitch.

Plain-English summaries

The writing is meant to help non-experts choose quickly without reading vendor jargon.

How updates work

The directory now tracks both review recency and new additions. The homepage freshness sections surface both so repeat visitors can see that the site is active.

Current coverage: 31 tools, 13 jobs, and 9 comparison pages.

How monetization is handled

Some pages may use partner links. That does not change the editorial rule: if a tool is a weak fit, the page should say so. Partner links are disclosed instead of hidden.

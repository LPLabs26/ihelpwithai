Otter

A simple meeting note-taker for transcripts, summaries, and searchable call records.

What it’s for

Use Otter when you mainly want transcripts and meeting notes in an interface that feels simple and familiar.

Who should use it

Best for teams, students, and operators who want less note-taking overhead.

Buyer guide

Best fit

Best for small teams, founders, and operators who mainly want dependable transcripts and simple meeting notes without a more opinionated sales or workflow layer.

Bad fit

Not ideal if you want stronger post-call automation, richer sales handoff support, or more structured meeting intelligence beyond transcription and summaries.

Pricing snapshot

Free to try and easy to justify for transcript-first needs, but buyers should compare whether they are paying for notes only or for a broader follow-up workflow.

Setup reality

Very easy to adopt. The main question is not setup difficulty but whether simple transcripts are enough for your workflow or whether the team will outgrow it quickly.

One real use case

Capture a customer interview, then search the transcript later for objections, requests, and next steps.

Why it made the directory

It keeps the meeting workflow straightforward and lowers the chance of losing useful details.

Watch-outs

If you want more advanced meeting intelligence or partner monetization paths, Fireflies may be stronger.

Pros and cons

Pros

  • Simple, familiar product for teams that want searchable transcripts without much learning curve.
  • Good fit when the core need is documentation, recall, and basic summaries.
  • Easier to roll out across mixed teams than tools with more specialized sales positioning.

Cons

  • Weaker as a full follow-up system than more workflow-oriented competitors.
  • Less differentiated if the team needs action tracking, CRM handoff, or call coaching.
  • Can become just a transcript archive if there is no clear follow-up process.

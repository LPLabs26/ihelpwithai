Transparency matters

Affiliate disclosure

Some links on ihelpwithai.com may become partner links over time. If that happens, the site may earn a commission when someone buys through that link, at no extra cost to the reader.

How recommendations work

Tools are chosen because they solve a real job clearly, not because they pay the highest commission. Fit comes first. Revenue comes second.

What readers should expect

If a page uses a partner link, the call-to-action may point to that tracked link instead of the default official URL. The tool page will still explain what the product is good at, who it is for, and where it may not be the best fit.

What this site is trying to be

This is meant to be a practical AI buyer’s guide in plain English. The goal is to reduce confusion, speed up decisions, and help people avoid buying tools that do not match the work they actually need done.

Editorial rules

Plain-English before hype

Every tool should be understandable to someone who is not already deep in AI.

Real use cases

Recommendations need concrete examples, not just feature lists.

Tradeoffs stay visible

Watch-outs and better-fit alternatives matter just as much as the pitch.

Service businesses are drowning in software demos, vendor hype, and generic "top 10 AI tools" lists that were written for nobody. This site exists to fix that.

Why this site exists

I kept watching service business owners — HVAC techs, salon owners, contractors, barbers — get sold the wrong software. Not because they made bad decisions, but because every source of information was either a vendor trying to close a deal or a generic blog post with no actual context about how these businesses run.

The guy running a 4-person plumbing shop doesn't need the same software as a 50-location franchise. A solo lash artist has different problems than a medspa with six providers. Most buyer's guides don't acknowledge this. They list options and leave you to figure it out.

This site is built around a different approach: start with the problem, match the tool to how the business actually runs, and be honest about who something is not right for — not just who it's great for.

How recommendations work

Every pick on this site is evaluated against the same set of questions:

🎯

What problem does this actually solve?

Not "what does it do" — what specific pain does it fix, and how well does it fix it compared to alternatives?

⚙️

What's the real setup burden?

Vendor onboarding materials are optimistic. The reviews here try to reflect what actually happens when a busy business owner or office manager tries to get something running.

🚫

Who should not use this?

Every review includes a bad-fit section. If a tool is wrong for certain situations, that's as important to say as when it's right.

💰

Is the pricing realistic for this business type?

A solo beauty pro and a 20-person HVAC shop have different budgets. Pricing posture is always surfaced — not just the starting plan price.

🔄

What are the alternatives?

No review ends with just one option. If there's a better fit for a specific situation, it gets mentioned.

What this site is not

It's not a directory. There are hundreds of AI tools and service-business software products — this site covers a focused set of well-vetted options, not everything that exists.

It's not an AI news site or a hype machine. New tools get added when they've been around long enough to evaluate honestly — not when a press release goes out.

It's not built for enterprise. The two verticals here — field trades and beauty/wellness — are focused on small to mid-size owner-operated businesses. If you're running a 500-person operation, some of these picks will feel lightweight.

Best AI tools for meetings and notes

If the pain is losing details after calls or spending too much time writing follow-up notes, these are the smartest first tools to compare.

What this guide helps with

Start here for transcripts, meeting summaries, action items, and cleaner follow-up after calls.

Best tools to compare first

Workspace ClickUp Free to try

ClickUp Brain

A workspace AI layer that helps across docs, tasks, meeting notes, summaries, and finding answers inside your work system.

Use it when: Use ClickUp Brain when you want AI embedded directly in the place your team already manages work instead of using a separate tool for every step.

Real use case: Summarize a project thread, generate an update for leadership, and turn the next steps into assigned tasks.

Video Descript Free to try

Descript

Best for editing spoken content because it lets you edit audio and video like a document.

Use it when: Use Descript to record, transcribe, cut filler words, add captions, and publish polished content quickly.

Real use case: Drop in a webinar recording and turn it into cleaned-up clips with captions for social media.

Meetings Fathom Free to try

Fathom

A very approachable AI meeting assistant for notes, action items, summaries, and cleaner post-call follow-up.

Use it when: Use Fathom when your biggest problem is losing time after meetings and you want the notes, highlights, and next steps handled fast.

Real use case: Turn a customer call into a clean summary, a short follow-up email, and a list of next actions for the owner.

Meetings Fireflies.ai Free to try

Fireflies

A strong meeting assistant for recording calls, pulling summaries, and keeping follow-ups from slipping.

Use it when: Use Fireflies when you want automatic notes, searchable transcripts, and meeting follow-up help without manual admin work.

Real use case: After every discovery call, create a summary, action list, and CRM-ready handoff without doing it by hand.

Video Loom Free to try

Loom

A strong first pick for async explanation because it makes screen recording, sharing, and AI summaries feel simple.

Use it when: Use Loom when you need to explain something once on video instead of repeating the same instructions in meetings or long messages.

Real use case: Record a product walkthrough once, then turn it into a shareable update, a summary, and a reusable SOP.

Meetings Krisp Free to try

Krisp

A practical choice for cleaner meetings because it mixes noise control, transcripts, notes, and AI meeting help in one place.

Use it when: Use Krisp when audio quality is part of the pain, not just note taking, and you want meetings to sound cleaner while still capturing the takeaways.
